Let's Learn Geometry!

2D Shapes

2D shapes are flat shapes. Think of it like a piece of paper, flat!

There are two main types of shapes; polygons and non-polygons.


Non-Polygons

A shape that is either not connected by their side or has curved sides.
An example is a circle. A circle is connected, HOWEVER it is curved and does NOT have straight sides.

Polygons

Polygons are 2D shapes that have straight sides which are connected creating vertices. The main types of polygons include triangles, quadrilaterals, (including square and rectangles), pentagon, hexagon.

Triangles

A polygon with 3 angles and 3 connected straight sides.

Quadrilaterals

a polygon with 4 connected, straight sides and 4 angles.

Pentagons

A polygon with 5 connected, straight sides and 5 angles.


Hexagon

A polygon with 6 connected, straight sides and 6 angles.

3D Figures

3D figures are quite different from 2D. We talked about 2D shapes being like paper, flat. Well 3D, or 3 dimensional, figures can be held, tossed and you can see all the sides!

Some of the most common 3D figures include spheres, cones, cylinders, rectangular prisms, and pyramids. 3D figures are either curved or have faces, edges, and vertices.

  • Faces: flat or curved surface
  • Edges: where two faces meet
  • Vertices: where 3 vertices meet

Sphere: A 3D figure that has 0 faces, 0 vertices, and it's surface is curved.

Cone: A 3D figure that has 2 faces (1 is curved and the other is in the shape of a circle), 1 vertices, and it's surface is curved.

Cylinders: these have 2 faces in the shape of a circle and one curved face, like soup, each top and bottom is the shape of a circle.

Pyramids: has a base that is a square face, and 4 triangle faces that meet at the apex.

Rectangular Prism: this has 6 faces, each the shape of a rectangle with 12 edges and 8 vertices. (pic of tissue box)

2D vs. 3D shapes

REMEMBER

  1. 2D shapes are flat!
  2. 2D shapes do not have depth.
  3. 3D figures have faces, edges, and/or vertices.
  4. 3D figures have length, width, depth.
